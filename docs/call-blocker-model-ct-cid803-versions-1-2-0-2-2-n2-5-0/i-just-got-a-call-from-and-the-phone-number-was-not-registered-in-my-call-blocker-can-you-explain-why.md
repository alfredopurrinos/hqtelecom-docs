First, Please keep in mind that your call blocker may not to be able to register all the incoming calls the same way your handset registers them mainly because they have different caller id reading software.

At times, a call blocker device is not able to register an incoming call mainly because it cannot validate the name or the phone number received from the calling party.  Also, sneaky telemarketers may alter their call id information to prevent the call blockers from blocking them.  In our experience, this happens very seldom and mostly on cell phone callers.    
  
In essence, these calls are treated by your call blocker the same way it treats the UNKNOWN, PRIVATE, OUT OF AREA calls.  You can turn feature # 6 to block these calls, however, this may also block legit calls.  Please refer to this article for more details:  
[https://rentalo.freshdesk.com/solution/articles/5000626937-pros-cons-of-feature-set-6-blocking-calls-with-no-caller-id](/call-blocker-model-ct-cid803-versions-1-2-0-2-2-n2-5-0/pros-cons-of-feature-set-6-blocking-calls-with-no-caller-id)  
  
Ref: <https://www.hqtelecom.com/TLock-CallBlocker-Manual-V5.pdf>
