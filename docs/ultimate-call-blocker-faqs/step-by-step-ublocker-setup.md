Congratulations!!! on your purchase of a Ultimate Call Blocker (UBlocker).  Please follow this step by step instructions to setup your UBlocker. These instructions apply to UBlocker LAN and WIFI versions.

---

Step 1.  Connecting your phoneline  
  
a. Locate the device labeled as "**Phone Adapter"** in the package.   
  
 – Allows the UBlocker to connect to your landline phone jack or modem port.  
   
– Plugs into any of the four USB ports on the UBlocker.  
   
– Includes two RJ11 sockets:  
  
  • **LINE:** Connects to your wall jack or modem port.  
  
  • **PHONE:** Connects to your phone or phone system.  
  
 – An RJ11 patch cord is included. 

b. Connect the USB plug on the **Phone Adapter** to **any the 4 USB ports** available in the UBlocker (as shown in diagram below).    
  
c. On the other side of the **Phone Adapter**, there are 2 phone sockets (or holes e.g., RJ11 sockets).  One labeled "LINE", and the other labeled "PHONE".   Plug in a phone cable (one is included in the package) to the socket labeled "LINE" on the **Phone Adapter.**Then, plug in the other side of the phone cable to the**phone line**(e.g., wall jack or modem port). 

d. Next, you can plug in your existing phone system (handset or base) to the socket labeled "**PHONE**" on the **Phone Adapter.**

Important:  This know that it is not required to have a phone connected to the Phone Adapter of the UBlocker in order for the UBlocker to work properly, however you will need a phone connected to the phone adapter in order to make sure you have dial tone going to the Phone Adapter.  After you finish the UBlocker setup, you can unplug the phone and use it on any other wall jack, if desired.  
  
![](https://s3.amazonaws.com/cdn.freshdesk.com/data/helpdesk/attachments/production/13189899997/original/0fp_pEDA9luokl1BNzGXed0YN5vzx3yVbA.png?1761454858)

**Important:**

- The Phone Adapter has **two RJ11 sockets** labeled **LINE** and **PHONE**.
- Plug the **LINE** port into the wall jack or modem port (the one with a dial tone) using an RJ11 telephone patch cable.
- If desired, connect your existing telephone (base or handset) to the **PHONE** port using another RJ11 patch cable (existing).
- A phone connection is **optional** — the UBlocker will still function correctly even without one connected.
- The **Phone Adapter** has a **red** led ON at all times indicating that it has power. It also has an intermittent **green** led ON indicating activity (off hook, answering or making calls).

Step 2.  Powering your UBlocker device  
  
Plug the **Power Adapter** (included) into the **micro USB port** on the UBlocker, as shown below.  
Then, connect the power adapter to a **standard 120V wall outlet**. 

![](https://s3.amazonaws.com/cdn.freshdesk.com/data/helpdesk/attachments/production/13189900003/original/zpcG0XTKD-KZVi3C56lNgoC4fi1ech--iA.png?1761454883)

**Important:**

- For best performance and protection, connect the UBlocker to a **UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)**.  
   → This helps prevent damage and extends the life of your device during power interruptions.
- Some power adapter models have a **red** led light ON indicating power is ON.
- Recommendation: If order to protect your UBlocker equipment as well as extend its lifespan, we recommend you use a UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) to plug in the power adapter.  If a UPS is not available, use a **surge-protected outlet** to guard against power surges.

Step 3. Connecting your UBlocker to the Internet

Your UBlocker can connect to the Internet in **two ways**:

1. **LAN / Ethernet**
2. **Wi-Fi**

> **Note:** We recommend you use a LAN / Ethernet connection in order to provide a more reliable connection.

#### **LAN / Ethernet Setup**

Follow these steps to connect your UBlocker directly to your router using an Ethernet cable.  
If you have a UBlocker version WIFI or WL and need to use a **Wi-Fi**connection, you may skip this section and go to **Step 4**.

1. Take the **included Ethernet cable** and plug one end into your **router**.  
   - Your router (or modem) should have at least 4 Ethernet ports available to allow connecting other devices e.g., desktop PC, laptop, TV, etc.
2. Plug the other end into the **Ethernet port** on your UBlocker.

![](https://s3.amazonaws.com/cdn.freshdesk.com/data/helpdesk/attachments/production/13189900009/original/MojH7-T-a7r4s9KsifN3FHu47-vgy9ECDg.png?1761454931)

Step 4. WIFI setup (optional, only apply to UBlocker WIFI or WL models)  
  
PLEASE SKIP THIS STEP IF YOU ALREADY HAVE YOUR UBLOCKER CONNECTED DIRECTLY TO YOUR ROUTER WITH AN ETHERNET CABLE (e.g. completed Step 3 above).

Please connect your UBlocker to the Internet via **Wi-Fi****only if**you are not able to connect your UBlocker directly to your router with an Ethernet cable.

Important:  Please make sure your UBlocker supports WIFI or WL. If you have a UBlocker LAN version, please skip this step.

**Preparation:**

- You need a **Wi-Fi-enabled device** (smartphone, tablet, or laptop).
- You will also need your **Wi-Fi router’s SSID** and **password** to complete the setup.

  - The **SSID** is the name of your router as it appears under **“My Networks”** or **“Other Networks”** on your Wi-Fi-enabled device.
  - **Important:** The SSID is **case-sensitive**, so type it exactly as shown.
  - If your router’s SSID is very long, it may be **truncated** in the network list. In that case, check the **label on your router** or contact your **Internet Service Provider (ISP)** for the full name.

    ⚠️ Caution: Most Wi-Fi setup failures occur because the SSID is entered incorrectly.
- **Important:** If you connected your UBlocker to your router with an Ethernet cable, unplug the Ethernet cable from the UBlocker before starting the Wi-Fi setup. You can reconnect it after you finish the Wi-Fi setup, if desired.

#### **4a) Connect to the UBlocker Network**

1. On your Wi-Fi device, go to **Settings > Wi-Fi**.
2. Wait for a network named **“UBlocker”** to appear under available networks.
3. Select **UBlocker** and enter the password: **ultimateblocker**
4. Once connected, close the Wi-Fi settings.

> **Note:** It may show “No Internet” — this is normal.

#### **4b) Configure your home Wi-Fi**

1. Open a browser (e.g., Safari, Chrome, Firefox, IE) on your Wi-Fi device.
2. Enter **<http://192.168.2.1>** in the address bar and press **Enter**.
3. On the page that appears, enter your **Wi-Fi router’s SSID** and **password**.
4. Press the **“Update Wi-Fi”** button.

> **Important:** Both SSID and password are **case-sensitive**. Enter the SSID (e.g. name) of your router exactly as it appears in your WIFI available networks list.

![](https://s3.amazonaws.com/cdn.freshdesk.com/data/helpdesk/attachments/production/13189899552/original/_QlFmfrzc6ZofSvIC0SiyKKfQKOeENAS1A.png?1761448824)

Please type your WIFI router’s SSID and Password of your WIFI router and press the “Update Wi-Fi” button. Important:  Please make sure you type the SSID exactly as you see it under “Available networks”.  Both entries are case sensitive.

### **Tips & Troubleshooting**

1. After powering up your UBlocker for the first time, it may take a few minutes (up to 15 minutes) for **“UBlocker”** to appear in your network list. Please be patient.
2. Make sure to type **“http://”** before **192.168.2.1** in step 4b.
3. Ignore browser messages like **“This site can’t be reached”** after pressing **Update Wi-Fi**. At this point, your UBlocker is switching from your device to your Wi-Fi router.
4. Complete this setup within **5 minutes**. Otherwise, the UBlocker will reboot and you may need to redo the process.
5. Once the UBlocker connects to your Wi-Fi router, **“UBlocker”** will no longer appear in your device’s available networks.

#### **Step 6. Testing your device's setup**

After completing the setup above, follow these simple instructions to make sure your UBlocker is installed and working properly.

1. Use your mobile phone or another phone line to make a **test call** to the number connected to your UBlocker.
2. Let the call **ring 2–3 times**, then hang up.
3. Log in to your account at **<https://ultimatecallblocker.com>** and press **Refresh** on your browser.
4. Scroll to the **“Call Log”** section and check if your test call has been recorded.

> ✅ If your test call appears in the Call Log, your UBlocker is set up correctly — congratulations! You have completed the setup of your UBlocker.

#### **Troubleshooting**

If the test call is **not logged**, follow these steps:

1. Ensure the **phone line is connected properly.**

   1. Refer to step 1 above to review the Phone Adapter connections.
   2. Plug in your phone to the PHONE socket on the Phone Adapter and make sure you hear dial tone.
2. Verify that the **UBlocker is properly connected to the Internet**.

   1. If you are using an Ethernet cable to connect the UBlocker directly into your router, make sure you have a blinking green light (activity light) on both; the UBlocker's Ethernet port (check LEDs below the socket), and also on your router.
   2. If you can, please log to your router and check the connected devices to make sure there is one showing as "UBlocker".  (Note:  This may required help from your Internet service provider.)
   3. If you have another device connected to your router via Ethernet cable, try swapping the ports to see if you get connectivity as some ISPs may disable unused Ethernet ports).
   4. If you are using a Wi-Fi connection, please check your Wi-Fi enabled device to make sure the UBlocker is not showing under "My Networks" or "Other Networks".  If the UBlocker is still showing, this means that your UBlocker is not connecting to the Internet yet.  Please go over step 4 above again and make sure you enter the correct SSID and password.
3. **Power cycle** your UBlocker device (unplug the power adapter, wait 30 seconds and then plug it back on).

If the problem persists after these steps, [contact](https://www.hqtelecom.com/pages/contact) our **technical support team [here](https://www.hqtelecom.com/pages/contact)**

- Include your **name**, **phone number**, **best time to call**, and login info (**username** or **registered email**).
- A technician will assist you over the phone, usually within **1 business day**.
